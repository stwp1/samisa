ANGOLA, DR CONGO, EGYPT QUALIFY, SOUTH AFRICA DENIED BY CONGO

Angola, DR Congo, and Egypt have successfully secured their spots in the upcoming tournament, showcasing strong performances throughout the qualifiers. However, South Africa faced a major setback as their hopes were dashed by a resilient Congo team, leaving them out of the competition. Despite a determined effort, South Africa couldn't break through, while Congo’s strategic play denied them the much-needed qualification. The spotlight now shifts to the qualifying nations as they prepare for the next stage, while South Africa is left to reflect on what went wrong.

TotalEnergies CAF Africa Cup of Nations Morocco 2025 qualifiers action on Tuesday saw more nations secure their place in the finals as the race towards a place in Morocco continues to intensify across the continent.

  • Rwanda 2-1 Benin: Rwanda kept their qualification hopes alive with a vital win.
  • Mauritania 0 - 1 Egypt: Egypt maintained their winning streak with a narrow win.
  • Tanzania 0 – 2 DR Congo: DR Congo sealed their place with an impressive win.
  • Niger 0 – 1 Angola: A strike in the opening minute secured Angola's place.
  • Botswana 1 – 0 Cape Verde: Botswana edged Cape Verde to keep their chances alive.
  • Sierra Leone 1 – 0 Cote d’Ivoire: Cote d’Ivoire suffered a surprising defeat.
  • Guinea-Bissau 0 – 0 Mali: Mali secured a point to maintain their position.
  • Congo 1 – 1 South Africa: A thrilling draw kept South Africa's hopes alive.
